Executive Overview
AI implementation is underway at Retail Co. This dashboard identifies which departments have the largest stakeholder engagement gap — and who to prioritize for intervention.

Dashboard Purpose: Who needs stakeholder engagement most?

Stakeholder Engagement Action Plan
Evidence-based recommendations derived from the quadrant analysis. Each action is tied directly to survey findings.
🔴
Priority 1 — Immediate Engagement: Warehouse & Logistics + Finance
Why: Both departments score high on data quality perception (4.4 and 4.3) but critically low on training adequacy (1.4 and 2.3). They understand why AI matters — they just have no support. This is the highest-ROI engagement window.
Action: Assign dedicated AI implementation liaisons. Launch role-specific training within 30 days. Manager briefings required before employee training begins.
✅
Priority 2 — Leverage Champions: IT + Marketing
Why: Both departments score high on training adequacy (4.6 and 3.7) AND data quality perception (4.5 and 4.4). They are already operating effectively with AI.
Action: Designate IT and Marketing employees as peer mentors for cross-departmental AI support. Pair them with Warehouse & Logistics and Finance teams in structured knowledge-transfer sessions.
🟡
Priority 3 — Foundation Building: Customer Service + Store Operations
Why: Both score low on training AND data quality perception. They don't yet see the value of AI or data-driven decisions. Throwing training at them without awareness-building will fail.
Action: Begin with awareness workshops — use real examples from their own workflows. Only introduce tools after perception of data value improves. Timeline: 60–90 days.

Survey Design
5 questions, each producing a 1–5 score, designed to enable multi-dimensional stakeholder engagement analysis. The two primary axes (Q2 and Q4) power the quadrant model.
Q1 — AI Readiness
On a scale of 1 to 5, how ready do you feel to adopt and use AI tools in your current role at work?
1 = Not ready at all · 5 = Fully ready and confident
Purpose: Establishes baseline capability level per department. Used in radar and deep-dive comparisons.
Q2 — Training Adequacy ← Primary Axis X
How adequate is the AI-related training and support your department has received in preparation for this implementation?
1 = No training provided at all · 5 = Comprehensive, role-specific training received
Purpose: X-axis of the engagement quadrant. Low scores identify departments with unmet support needs.
Q3 — Change Readiness
How open and prepared is your team to adapt to the organizational changes that come with AI implementation?
1 = Strongly resistant to change · 5 = Fully embracing the transition
Purpose: Identifies resistance risk. Low change readiness combined with low training predicts adoption failure.
Q4 — Data Quality Perception ← Primary Axis Y
How well does your team understand the quality, relevance, and strategic value of the data your department works with?
1 = Little to no awareness of data value · 5 = Strong understanding of data quality and its business impact
Purpose: Y-axis of the engagement quadrant. High score + low training = highest-priority engagement target (Yvonne's example).
Q5 — Current Stakeholder Engagement Level
How engaged do you feel as a stakeholder in the AI implementation process — including communication, decision-making involvement, and visibility into project progress?
1 = Completely excluded from the process · 5 = Actively involved and well-informed throughout
Purpose: Directly measures the engagement gap this dashboard is designed to close. Used in action plan projections.
